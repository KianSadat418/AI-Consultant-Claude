# /founder synthesis -- Executive Strategy Summary

## Purpose
Read all existing output files generated by other founder skills and synthesize them into a McKinsey senior partner-level executive strategy document. This is the final deliverable: the document you'd present to a board of directors.

## Trigger
User types: `/founder synthesis`

## Data Retrieval

1. **Load CONTEXT.md** if present.

2. **Scan the working directory** for all existing founder skill outputs:
   - `MARKET-SIZING.md`
   - `INDUSTRY-TRENDS.md`
   - `COMPETITIVE-LANDSCAPE.md`
   - `CUSTOMER-PERSONAS.md`
   - `SWOT-ANALYSIS.md`
   - `GTM-PLAYBOOK.md`
   - `PRICING-STRATEGY.md`
   - `RISK-ASSESSMENT.md`
   - `CUSTOMER-JOURNEY.md`
   - `UNIT-ECONOMICS.md`
   - `FINANCIAL-MODEL.md`
   - `FUNDRAISING-STRATEGY.md`
   - `PITCH-DECK-BRIEF.md`
   - `LEGAL-STRUCTURE.md`
   - `EQUITY-STRUCTURE.md`
   - `MARKET-ENTRY.md`

3. **Load all that exist.** Note which are missing. The synthesis should work with whatever is available, but flag gaps.

4. If fewer than 3 analysis files exist, warn the user: "For a comprehensive synthesis, run more individual analyses first. Available: /founder tam, /founder compete, /founder swot, etc."

## Analysis Framework

### McKinsey Senior Partner Presentation Structure

Write as if you are a McKinsey senior partner presenting to the board after your team spent a week conducting deep analysis. The tone should be: confident, direct, evidence-based, strategically sophisticated, and honest about risks.

**1. Executive Summary (5 paragraphs)**
- Paragraph 1: What the company is and its current strategic position
- Paragraph 2: The market opportunity (size, growth, timing)
- Paragraph 3: Competitive dynamics and the company's edge (or lack thereof)
- Paragraph 4: Financial trajectory and key inflection points
- Paragraph 5: The single most important strategic choice facing the company

**2. Current State Assessment**
Be brutally honest. Where does the company actually stand?
- Product-market fit assessment (strong/emerging/unproven)
- Competitive position (leader/challenger/follower/niche)
- Financial health (strong/adequate/concerning)
- Team readiness (ready to scale/needs key hires/gaps)
- Overall readiness score with justification

**3. Three Strategic Paths**
Present 3 distinct strategic options:

- **Conservative**: Lower risk, slower growth, capital efficient. Prioritize profitability and organic growth. Good if: market is uncertain, capital is expensive, product needs refinement.

- **Balanced**: Moderate risk, measured growth, strategic investment. The "Goldilocks" path. Good if: product-market fit is emerging, capital is available, team is solid.

- **Aggressive**: Higher risk, fast growth, capital intensive. Prioritize market share and speed. Good if: market is heating up, window is closing, strong competitive moats exist.

For each path:
- Strategic approach in 3-4 sentences
- Expected outcome (revenue, customers, position) at 12 and 24 months
- Investment required (capital, team, time)
- Timeline to key milestones
- Key risks and how they're mitigated
- What needs to be true for this path to work

**4. Recommended Strategy**
State clearly which path to take and why. This is not a "it depends" section. Make a call. Back it with evidence from the analyses.

**5. Top 5 Priority Initiatives (Next 90 Days)**
For each:
- What to do (specific, actionable)
- Why it matters (strategic rationale)
- Expected outcome
- Resources required
- Owner (role, not name)
- Success metric

**6. Resource Requirements**
What the company needs to execute the recommended strategy:
- Capital (how much, when, from whom)
- People (what roles, when to hire)
- Technology (what to build, buy, or integrate)
- Partnerships (who to align with)

**7. Decision Framework**
For the next 10 strategic decisions the founder will face, provide a framework:
- Hiring: When to hire ahead of revenue vs. stay lean
- Product: When to add features vs. focus
- Market: When to expand vs. deepen
- Fundraising: When to raise vs. bootstrap
- Pricing: When to change pricing
- Competition: When to respond vs. ignore
- Partnerships: When to partner vs. go alone

**8. "If I Only Had 1 Hour" Brief**
Compress the entire synthesis into a 1-page brief:
- 3 sentences: what the company is
- 3 sentences: why the market is attractive
- 3 sentences: the strategic recommendation
- 3 sentences: what to do in the next 90 days
